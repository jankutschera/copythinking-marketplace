# Sexified Bullet-Point Formulas

Bullet points are simple one-sentence hooks that break up text, create curiosity, and build desire. Transform basic bullet points into compelling copy that makes readers eager to buy.

## Core Principles

- Bullet points make information easier to read by creating pattern interrupts
- They should create curiosity AND build desire
- Never just list features - transform them into emotional hooks
- Each bullet point should feel like a mini sales pitch

## The 19 Formulas

### #1 The "Wrong!" Bullet
**Formula:** Common assumption → Unique contradiction
**Pattern:** "You think X is good! But you're wrong!"

Example:
> Hand-copying is the best way to practice copywriting. Right? Wrong! Discover why hand-copying successful sales pages will REVERSE your progress.

### #2 The "Two-Step" Bullet
**Formula:** Start with benefit → Inject fear
**Pattern:** "What to never do when X and why. (If you do this wrong you're risking Y)"

Example:
> What to never do when you start advertising on Facebook and why. (If you get this wrong you could have your ad account banned and you'll never be able to advertise again)

### #3 The "Giveaway" Bullet
**Formula:** Give away valuable information to position as expert
**Pattern:** Say something valuable directly

Example:
> The easiest way to get better at copywriting is finding someone who needs to build a sales page and do it for free

### #4 The "Reverse Hook" Bullet
**Formula:** Information → Turn into hook
**Pattern:** "(Information) Learn how to leverage (the information) to get X."

Example:
> There are millions of accounts on Twitter but only 2% will ever reach 1,000 followers. Learn how to leverage the Twitter algorithm to build a following FAST.

### #5 The "Naked Benefit" Bullet
**Formula:** Straightforward benefit statement
**Pattern:** "How to get (desired result)"

Example:
> How to ensure to not get closed on FB ads when running long-form sales pages.

### #6 The "Transactional" Bullet
**Formula:** Exchange proposition
**Pattern:** "Give me X and I'll give you Y"

Example:
> Give me 10 minutes and I'll teach you how to write sales pages from scratch.

### #7 The "If... Then..." Bullet
**Formula:** Simple qualifier → Big result
**Pattern:** "If you (SIMPLE qualifier) then you can (get big result)"

Example:
> If you have a pen and paper at home right now then you can write a book within a week.

### #8 The "Truth About" Bullet
**Formula:** Target controversial topics
**Pattern:** "The truth about (subject). They are to blame."

Example:
> The truth about using images on a sales page. HINT: professional copywriters have lied to you

### #9 The "Single Most" Bullet
**Formula:** Single out ONE superior element
**Pattern:** "Learn the most important (thing) about (subject)"

Example:
> Learn the most important element about writing long-form sales pages.

### #10 The "How-To" Bullet
**Formula:** Straightforward how-to with big claim
**Pattern:** "How to do (something) in (time frame)"

Example:
> How to write long-form sales pages in 30 minutes

### #11 The "Number" Bullet
**Formula:** Use specific numbers
**Pattern:** "The X reasons for Y"

Example:
> The 3 biggest reasons why your sales pages don't convert

### #12 The "Sneaky" Bullet
**Formula:** Conspiracy angle - someone big doing something hidden
**Pattern:** "The sneaky way (someone big) is doing X"

Example:
> The sneaky way professional copywriters are writing sales pages without doing any market research

### #13 The "Better Than" Bullet
**Formula:** Comparison to existing solution
**Pattern:** "How to do X better than Y"

Example:
> How to write enticing stories better than Hollywood does

### #14 The "Simple Fact" Bullet
**Formula:** State worst case → Offer solution
**Pattern:** "(State fact). This (product) could make sure you do better."

Example:
> 99% of Twitter users don't make a single buck from their followers. This course could make sure you're not one of them.

### #15 The "What Never" Bullet
**Formula:** Create curiosity or paint fear
**Pattern:** "What never to do. (Benefit)"

Example:
> What never to do when doing market research. (This will save you 2-3 hours for every sales page you build)

### #16 The "Do You?" Bullet
**Formula:** Point out problem → Offer solution
**Pattern:** "Do you (do something wrong)?"

Example:
> Do you get writer's block?

### #17 The "Reason Why" Bullet
**Formula:** Show you know the answer
**Pattern:** "The reason why (something bad happens)"

Example:
> The reason you're not getting your DMs answered.

### #18 The "Secrets Of" Bullet
**Formula:** Build curiosity with unique offer
**Pattern:** "The secret of X without Y"

Example:
> The secret of doing market research without ever going to read a single forum comment.

### #19 The "Probing Question" Bullet
**Formula:** Build empathy and credibility
**Pattern:** "Do you have (bad emotion) about Y?"

Example:
> Do you worry about having your Twitter account banned and you losing all your followers?

## Implementation Tips

1. **Mix and match formulas** - Don't use the same formula repeatedly
2. **Focus on emotions** - Each bullet should trigger fear, desire, or curiosity
3. **Be specific** - Vague bullets don't convert
4. **Test combinations** - Some formulas work better for certain markets
5. **Keep them scannable** - Bullets should be quick to read and understand
